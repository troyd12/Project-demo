# 7. The Competitive Intelligence Brief

## Purpose
Generates a comprehensive competitive analysis on any company, team, or technology in under 5 minutes. Perfect for pre-call research, hiring manager prep, and understanding the landscape before sourcing.

## The Prompt

```
You are a competitive intelligence analyst specializing in the tech industry. Research and produce a comprehensive brief.

<subject>
Company/Topic: [COMPANY NAME or TECHNOLOGY or TEAM]
Context: [WHY YOU NEED THIS — e.g., "sourcing against them," "hiring manager meeting," "evaluating their tech stack"]
</subject>

Produce a competitive intelligence brief covering:

**Company Overview**
- What they do (2-3 sentences, plain English)
- Founded, HQ, size, funding stage / public status
- Recent news (last 6 months) — launches, layoffs, pivots, acquisitions

**Leadership & Org Structure**
- Key leaders in [RELEVANT FUNCTION]
- Team size estimates for the function I care about
- Recent leadership changes or reorgs

**Technology & Engineering**
- Primary tech stack (languages, frameworks, cloud providers)
- Open source contributions or engineering blog highlights
- Known technical challenges or architectural decisions

**Culture & Employer Brand**
- Glassdoor/Blind sentiment summary
- Known perks, compensation bands, remote policies
- What candidates from this company typically value

**Talent Movement Patterns**
- Where their people tend to go when they leave
- Where they typically hire from
- Average tenure signals

**Strategic Assessment**
- Strengths to highlight when recruiting against them
- Weaknesses or pain points candidates might have
- How to position [YOUR COMPANY] as a better opportunity

Include a confidence score (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) next to each section based on how current and reliable the information is.
```

## When to Use
- Before a hiring manager intake meeting
- When sourcing against a specific competitor
- Preparing for a candidate who's coming from that company
- GPS roles where you need to understand the facilities/architecture landscape

## Pro Tip
Follow up with: "Now give me 5 talk tracks for pitching Nike over [COMPETITOR] to a passive candidate."
